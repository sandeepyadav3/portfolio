Airport Gate Operations Platform (0->1)

End-to-end architecture and backend ownership of a mission-critical airport operations system.

Overview

I led the development of a production-grade airport gate operations platform built from scratch for an enterprise client. The system manages real-time gate assignments, integrates external aviation systems, and supports operational workflows across airport stakeholders.

The Problem

Airport operations relied on fragmented systems and manual coordination. The new platform needed to:

  • Ingest real-time flight data from external aviation systems
  • Integrate with financial and lease management platforms
  • Maintain reliability during peak operational loads
  • Support extensibility for multiple airport clients

My Role & Ownership

  • Designed the microservices architecture and defined service boundaries
  • Built backend services and REST APIs
  • Managed AWS infrastructure (EC2, S3, VPC, CodeDeploy)
  • Configured Nginx reverse proxies and production routing
  • Identified and resolved performance bottlenecks in production
  • Worked directly with stakeholders to translate operational needs into technical design

Architecture & Technical Approach

  • Microservices architecture with modular, independently scalable services
  • Clear separation of gate management, flight ingestion, financial integration, and configuration services
  • REST-based inter-service communication
  • Database indexing and query optimization for performance-sensitive workflows
  • Automation of repetitive operational processes

Performance & Impact

  • Improved backend performance by ~3x through workflow and database optimization
  • Eliminated recurring production slowdowns
  • Automated legacy module migration (12–16 hours -> under 2 minutes)
  • Reduced onboarding effort for new clients through configurable system settings
Business Outcome: Delivered a scalable, production-ready airport gate management platform with improved operational efficiency and increased engineering leverage.

Key Learnings

  • Clear service boundaries prevent scaling bottlenecks
  • Measure before optimizing — observability drives effective performance tuning
  • Automation creates leverage in small engineering teams
  • Architecture decisions must align with business constraints
